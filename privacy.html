Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 28, 2026
4CITE.ai is in closed beta. This Privacy Policy describes how 4 SHIELD LLC (the operator of 4CITE.ai) collects, uses, and shares information today. It will be updated before public launch and again whenever a material change occurs. For data-handling questions contact hello@4cite.ai.

1. Who we are

4CITE.ai is operated by 4 SHIELD LLC, a Wyoming Benefit LLC. References to "we," "us," "our," or "4CITE" in this policy mean 4 SHIELD LLC. References to "you" mean the individual or organization using 4CITE.ai.

2. What we collect

Information you give us

  • Account information. Email address (required for account access and delivery of certificates and receipts) and the vertical you indicate as your primary use case (law, business, or government).
  • Documents you submit for Scan, Report, or Certify. The text content of the documents you upload or paste into the product. Stored in your Private Vault under your account.
  • Payment information. When you purchase a 4CITE Cert or subscribe to a plan, payment details (card number, billing address, etc.) are collected directly by Stripe, Inc. on our behalf. We do not see or store your full card number. We receive only a customer ID, the last four digits of the card, and the brand of the card, together with the transaction record needed for billing reconciliation. See Section 6 — Subprocessors for more on Stripe's role.
  • Communications you send us. Email or contact-form messages you send to hello@4cite.ai or any other 4CITE address.

Information collected automatically

  • Usage data. Counts of Scans, Reports, and Certificates run, the timestamp of each action, and the unit cost — used for billing, rate limiting, and capacity planning. We do not retain document content beyond what is required to produce the requested analysis and to store any certificates you have issued.
  • Device and connection data. IP address, user-agent string, and approximate region — used for security, fraud prevention, and basic analytics.
  • API logs. When you call the 4CITE API with an issued key, we record the request path, response status, and unit cost. We do not log request bodies in plain form.

3. The three data planes

4CITE.ai keeps user-submitted text strictly separate from the public reference corpus. This separation is part of the product's correctness model, not just a privacy choice.

  • Public Corpus. Documents we have ingested from public sources (CourtListener / PACER, SEC EDGAR, Congress.gov, GovInfo.gov, the Federal Register, FEC). No user-submitted content is ever merged into the Public Corpus.
  • Public Registry. Verifiability records for issued Integrity Certificates — certificate ID, scoring metadata, timestamp, and signing hash. The Registry contains no source document content. It exists only so a third party can confirm a certificate was actually issued.
  • Private Vault. Your source documents and Integrity Certificates. Encrypted in transit and at rest. Visible only to you and to authorized 4 SHIELD LLC personnel acting on your behalf for support or security purposes. You control deletion.

4. Research Corpus opt-in

The Research Corpus is a separate, opt-in data plane used to support continued calibration of the scoring engine. Inclusion is off by default. The opt-in checkbox appears only at the Certify step and must be actively selected each time you choose to contribute. Contributions are anonymized at the source-text level before any merge into the research plane. Research Corpus contributions are never merged with benchmark queries and are never used as training data without a separate, document-specific consent step.

5. How we use information

  • To operate the service — run Scans, Reports, and Certify pipelines you request.
  • To bill you for usage and to reconcile billing events with our payment processor.
  • To deliver certificates, receipts, magic-link logins, and account-related notifications.
  • To prevent abuse, fraud, and unauthorized access.
  • To improve the engine through aggregate, non-attributable usage analysis. We do not use your private documents to train or fine-tune any model.
  • To comply with applicable law, valid legal process, or to enforce our Terms of Service.

7. Sharing and disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share your documents with advertisers. We disclose information only as follows:

  • To the subprocessors listed above, for the purposes described.
  • To comply with applicable law, valid legal process, or a government request that we reasonably believe is lawfully issued.
  • To enforce our Terms of Service, protect the rights, property, or safety of 4 SHIELD LLC, our users, or the public, or to detect or prevent fraud, abuse, or security incidents.
  • In connection with a corporate transaction (merger, acquisition, financing, sale of assets) — in which case we will provide notice and the acquirer will be bound by the same privacy commitments or you will be given a chance to delete your data first.

8. Retention and deletion

Documents in your Private Vault are retained until you delete them. The system never unilaterally deletes Vault content while your account is active. You can delete individual documents, individual certificates, or close your account at any time from your account settings.

Issued Integrity Certificates have a permanent record in the Public Registry (certificate ID, hash, timestamp, scoring metadata) for verifiability. The Registry contains no source text. Deleting a certificate from your Vault removes the document content but does not retract the Registry record — that is the point of the Registry.

Account closure. When you close your account, Vault content is deleted within thirty (30) days. Billing records are retained for the period required by tax and financial-record-keeping laws (typically seven years). Public Registry records remain.

9. Security

Documents are transmitted to our service over TLS and stored encrypted at rest in AWS S3 under accounts owned by 4 SHIELD LLC. Production credentials are held in AWS Secrets Manager with restricted IAM access. Database access is gated through Supabase RLS policies tied to your account.

No system is perfectly secure. If we become aware of a security incident materially affecting your data, we will notify you and the relevant authorities as required by applicable law.
